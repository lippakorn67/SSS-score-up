How a swap works

From your shelf to someone's schoolbag in four steps.

1

Create your account

Sign up with your school number, so everyone you trade with is a real student here.

2

Post what you've outgrown

Snap a photo of a book, uniform or anything reusable and list it in under a minute.

3

Request a swap

See something you need? Offer one of your items in return, or just ask nicely.

4

Meet & exchange

Accepted requests show each other's details. Meet up at school, check the items and trade.

Why it matters

💸

Save money

Textbooks and uniforms are expensive. Passing them down means younger students — and their families — spend less every term.

♻️

Waste less

Every swapped item is one less thing in the bin, and one less new thing that has to be manufactured, packaged and shipped.

🤝

Look out for each other

A school where things get passed down feels different. Older students help newer ones — on purpose, not by accident.

Why we built this

We were inspired by eBay, where second-hand things find new owners every day — and we wondered why that couldn't happen inside a school. So much of what students buy is used for one year, one term, or one growth spurt, and then sits in a cupboard.

Swap, Share, Sustain adapts that idea for our community: a safe, free place where students moving between grades can pass their well-kept books, uniforms and supplies to the students coming up behind them.
